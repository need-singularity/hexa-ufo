## n=6 uniqueness invariant (Δ₀-absolute theorem)

`σ(n)·φ(n) = n·τ(n) = 24` holds uniquely at **n=6** across the entire chain.
This proposition is **Π₀¹-arithmetical** — therefore **Δ₀-absolute**: it holds
identically in *every* mathematical universe, every Knuth-arrow degree, every
large-cardinal extension, even at 𝔚. Just as `2+2=4` is true in any universe,
so is `σ·φ = n·τ = 24 iff n=6`. **n=6 is the common fingerprint of mathematical
universe.** UFO exists only inside the n=6 regime — and that regime extends
to 𝔚 itself.

  §23.9 says *"navigation stage 🛸16 is a self-closure fixed point — beyond

  with **"In practice, extensible."** That `extensible` claim was concretely
  worked out in `canon/domains/physics/meta-closure-nav/` — preserving it
  here keeps the atlas's "extensible" footnote *self-contained and citable*.
